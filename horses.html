Horses: The Story of a Former Horse Girl™

Ok so I used to be a Horse Girl™. There's a difference between Horse Girls™ and girls who had horses, by the way. Horse Girls™ are girls obsessed with horses, but do not own any. If you have horses, it's normal to become kind of obsessed, so that's not what we're talking about here.

My best friend and I conspired to trick my parents into buying one of her horses in elementary school. It obviously didn't work, and it was a stupid plan. But I really wanted a horse.

Anyway, I had a book called The Complete Book of Horses and I was religiously planning which breed(s) I would have and how I would care for them.

Favorite Horse Breeds:

  • DartmoorWikipedia says this is a pony, but I remember riding a horse that the owner said was a Dartmoor and was definitely a full-sized horse. Maybe I just remember it being bigger because I was smaller?
  • FriesianThe wavy main and tail are pretty cool.
  • LipizzanerI think it was the grey-white color and the cool-sounding name. I have always gravitated towards non-brown horses.
  • AppaloosaSpecifically, I wanted a Snowflake Appaloosa.
  • PalominoThis is not a breed, but rather a genetic color. Nonetheless, I wanted a Palomino horse, regardless of the breed.
  • PercheronAt the time, it was not as well-known as the Clydesdale, so I liked it more.

Horse Breeds I Hate:

  • ArabianWhy the FUCK do they look like that?? It's like how humans inbred pugs and French bulldogs until they looked as stupid as possible and couldn't breathe. This horse is an affront to God.
  • DunThis is also not a breed, but rather a gene. The dark stripe down the back just looks so stupid.
  • American Quarter HorseBasic.

I stopped being a horse girl when I realized people were making fun of me for it. IDK man, I grew up in a rural small town and a lot of people were into the country scene, but because I didn't have the money to have a horse, I was weird for liking them. I think we need to be nicer to Horse Girls™ because if we're only making fun of the ones that don't have the money to have a real horse, that feels kind of classist, no? Maybe I'm just biased.
